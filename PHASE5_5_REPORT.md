### Mechanism-to-Profile Physics

- **Dampening (A):** SOM peaks at expected orientation, directly suppresses nearby units. Profile: Gaussian trough at center (A < 0), recovers to 0 at large distances.
- **Sharpening (B):** DoG SOM creates narrow excitation + broad inhibition. Profile: Mexican hat — positive peak at center, negative dip at intermediate distances, returns to 0 far away.
- **Center-surround (C):** Broad SOM suppression + narrow center excitation to L2/3. Profile: Offset Gaussian — positive center peak with persistent negative baseline at ALL distances (does not return to 0).

### Key Discriminator

The critical distinction between sharpening and center-surround: sharpening profiles return to zero at large angular distances (Mexican hat), while center-surround maintains persistent baseline suppression (offset Gaussian with C < 0). This is visible in the raw profiles:
- Sharpening: supp[0]=+1.20, supp[90°]=+0.01 (returns to ~0)
- Center-surround: supp[0]=+4.34, supp[90°]=-0.39 (persistent offset)

### Observation Model Findings

1. **Dampening and sharpening** are highly recoverable even at coarse granularity (4 voxels) and moderate noise (SNR=5) — near-perfect MVPA accuracy.
2. **Center-surround is harder to recover** at low SNR and coarse granularity — 2-way accuracy drops to ~50% (chance) at 4 voxels/SNR=1. This is expected: center-surround's multivariate signature (center excitation + surround suppression) requires finer spatial resolution to distinguish from dampening's global suppression.
3. At high SNR (20), all mechanisms reach perfect or near-perfect recovery across all voxel counts.
4. **This is itself a finding**: measurement-level (voxel) signatures diverge from unit-level truth for center-surround at low resolution, suggesting that fMRI studies with coarse voxels may have difficulty distinguishing center-surround from dampening.
