# Task #74 — β/W_q_gain dampening is structurally one-sided, and sharpening is not reachable with the mechanisms we built

## 1. What we set out to do

The V2 model is a laminar predictive-coding circuit intended to reproduce two published paradigms from the Bayesian-V1 literature: Kok et al. (2012, *Neuron*) — expectation-induced sharpening of orientation representations in human V1, measured by fMRI voxel decoding on 45°/135° probes following matching vs. non-matching cues — and Richter et al. (2018, *J. Neurosci.*) — expectation via predictive leader cues and BOLD amplitude reduction on expected stimuli. The plan in `docs/v4_model_spec_*.md` breaks this into a phase-2 predictive substrate (LGN → L4 → L2/3E/PV/SOM → H → prediction head, with Urbanczik–Senn, Vogels iSTDP, ThresholdHomeostasis, and rolling-window training) and a phase-3 task-specific learning stage that writes cue→memory weights on a frozen-sensory-core substrate. The phase-3 head was intended to hook a context-memory cue into L2/3E through learned weights so that Kok-style sharpening and Richter-style dampening could both emerge from one substrate under different cue regimes.

## 2. What we built this session

This session completed a β mechanism that gates L4→L23E feedforward multiplicatively by a per-cue gain buffer called `W_q_gain` (shape `[n_c=48, n_l23_e=256]`, clamped to `[0.1, 1.0]`). Forward-pass consumption lives at `src/v2_model/layers.py:680`: `ff_l4 = F.linear(l4_input, w_l4)` and then, if `q_t is not None`, `ff_l4 *= q_t @ self.W_q_gain`. Phase-3 Kok training in `scripts/v2/train_phase3_kok_learning.py` applies a three-factor rule `ΔW_q_gain[cue_id, :] = -η · cue_on · r_l23e · sign(matched)` during the cue epoch of each trial, so W_q_gain for an active cue is pushed down on units that fire strongly for the matching probe orientation. Training ran 15 000 trials (5 000 learning + 10 000 scan) on seed 42 from the validated substrate checkpoint at `checkpoints/v2/phase2/phase2_validated_substrate_s42/phase2_s42/step_3000.pt`, with `--enable-w-q-gain-rule --disable-fix-j-mh-exc`, producing `checkpoints/v2/phase3_kok_beta_full_s42/phase3_kok_s42.pt`. The learned W_q_gain signature is sharply localised: only the two active cue rows moved from unity, with cue 0 (mapped to 135°) and cue 1 (mapped to 45°) both driving down to the `0.1` lower clamp on pref-of-cue units (average gain `0.10` on the ±15° anchor-pref mask) while their nonpref averages sit near `0.71` (cue 0: `0.7009`, cue 1: `0.7108`). All other cue rows stayed at their all-ones initialisation.

## 3. What we measured

The Level 11 expectation-sign gate on this checkpoint passed cleanly. Running `scripts/v2/step3_beta_level11.py` with the validated probe-gated protocol, Δr = mean_r_matched − mean_r_mismatched landed at −0.306 (mean_r_matched = 0.2176, mean_r_mismatched = 0.5236). On the pref-of-cue population, pref_exp = 0.1793 and pref_unexp = 1.7027, a roughly 10× suppression of the actively-tuned units under matched cue; on the nonpref-of-cue population, nonpref_exp = 0.2406 and nonpref_unexp = 0.0404, a ~6× enhancement. Both the delta-r sign and the pref/nonpref asymmetry are in the "dampening-with-suppressed-pref" geometry: the cue actively turns the pref-of-probe units down to near-silent while the nonpref-of-probe units rise.

The full metric triad through `scripts/v2/eval_kok.py` with the newly-added `--probe-cue-gate` flag (necessary because the legacy helper was delivering `q_t = None` during the probe1 epoch and silently bypassing the β mechanism while rates were being recorded) gave the same picture with bootstrapped confidence. Energy row: mean_r_exp = 0.2419 and mean_r_unexp = 0.4820, with Δr = −0.2401 (sign correct); pref_exp = 0.2046 vs pref_unexp = 1.3568 on the localizer-split asymmetry, with a 95 % bootstrap CI of [−1.3439, −1.3429] well below zero. Shape row: population-weighted FWHM went from 51.51° under unexpected to 52.92° under expected — wider on matched, Δfwhm = +1.40°, CI [1.302, 1.493], permutation p = 0.001. Main SVM remains at 1.000 on every fold for expected/unexpected/all because 45° vs. 135° is linearly separable with any reasonable representation (this is documented in the eval_kok header as a structural harness artefact); fine-discrim SVM reads 0.000 at 2.5° tolerance because a 12-orientation localizer has 15°-spaced labels and the decoder cannot score below half that spacing. The classification conclusion of the triad, read per the metric-triad memory, was **dampening by energy and shape** — the primary decoding metric was unmeasurable at this operating point because the instrument was pinned by design, not because the effect was absent.

The fMRI-style pseudo-voxel decoder in `scripts/v2/eval_fmri_decoder.py` tried to break that main-SVM ceiling by introducing retinotopic voxel pooling (64 voxels = 4 voxels × 16 retino cells = 4 units per voxel, mean-pooled like BOLD within a voxel) with input-level Gaussian noise and contrast jitter. On the phase-2 substrate checkpoint, both the coarse 8-way decoder and the binary 45°/135° decoder saturated at 1.000 ± 0.000 at `noise=0.1, readout=50, n_voxels=64`, and LinearSVC selected the weakest regulariser on every fold — the substrate carries orientation information so robustly that Kok-style voxel pooling at that operating point does not unceiling anything. A six-cell sweep over `noise ∈ {0.5, 1.0, 2.0} × readout ∈ {5, 10}` at `contrast ∈ [0.1, 0.3]` found a clear transition band: at `noise=0.5, readout=5` the 8-way coarse decoder reads 0.70 ± 0.02 (just above the target window upper bound of 0.70); at `noise=1.0, readout=5` it drops to 0.26; and at `noise=2.0` it collapses to ~0.13 (chance for 8-way is 0.125). Taking `noise=0.5, readout=5` as the operating point, the phase-2 substrate decoder on 45°/135° binary settles at 0.92 CV — below ceiling and above chance, the regime where a real β effect on orientation read-out should be measurable.

The frozen-localizer Δdecode on the β checkpoint, implemented in `scripts/v2/eval_fmri_decoder_kok.py` with `--protocol frozen-localizer`, trains a LinearSVC on cue-free localizer trials (1 600 trials, `cue_mode=none`, same operating point) and then applies it frozen to cued-matched and cued-mismatched test sets. The result is dramatic and unambiguous: acc_matched = 0.000, acc_mismatched = 1.000, Δ = −1.000, CI [−1, −1], permutation p = 0.001. Every matched trial is classified as the OPPOSITE orientation; every mismatched trial is classified correctly. This is not a null; it is a mechanism-level sign inversion of the orientation read-out on matched trials.

## 4. The feedback-vs-decoder isolation check

To understand whether the dramatic Δ = −1 reflected real β perturbation of V1 voxels or a broken decoder, `scripts/v2/eval_feedback_decoder_isolation.py` collected the localizer, matched, and mismatched trial-level voxel matrices and reported three blocks. Q1 — per-voxel representation check — showed the feedback is alive. Baseline mean voxel activity was 0.44 with per-voxel trial std 0.024; matched vs. none produces a median |Δ| of 4.77 σ_trial across voxels, and 45 of 64 voxels cross the 2σ cue-effect threshold (43 for mismatched vs. none, same magnitude). Matched and mismatched voxel means are nearly identical to each other (RSA correlation 0.9994; only 0 of 64 voxels cross 2σ between them), which is the first clue that the mechanism is symmetric in some sense. Q2 — decoder readout check — gave a 0.92 ± 0.02 localizer CV accuracy, a decoder weight L2 norm of 24.45, and grand-mean cosines of the cue shifts against the decoder of +0.11 (matched) and +0.11 (mismatched). By the dispatched grand-mean cosine threshold of 0.15 that reads as "orthogonal". The per-class projections in the same block, however, tell a different story: on cue-free trials the decoder puts 45° at a projection mean of −2.09 and 135° at +0.57 (class_sep = −1.65, the baseline); under matched cue, 45° projects to +24.41 and 135° to −19.09, and the class_sep SIGN FLIPS to +1.998; under mismatched cue, 45° projects to −20.12 and 135° to +25.23, class_sep = −1.998 (same sign as baseline, magnitude multiplied by ~1.2, with projection standard deviation expanded 13×). The β perturbation is large and strongly aligned with the decoder axis on a class-dependent basis: the cue moves each probe class roughly ±22 units (about 0.9 × ‖w‖) along the decoder axis, but in a sign that flips with class identity. The grand-mean cosine averages the two opposing shifts and reads near zero, which is a measurement artefact of averaging before taking the cosine, not a statement that the β shift is orthogonal to w. The mechanical consequence: under matched cue, every 45° trial lands in the region the decoder trained to call 135°, and vice versa — the locked decoder classifies every matched trial as its opposite. Q3 Δ = −1.000 and the 0 % matched accuracy are not a broken decoder, they are a faithful read-out of a β mechanism that inverts the class-discriminating axis on matched trials.

## 5. The structural one-sidedness argument

Two independent structural features of the β mechanism force the dampening-only direction. First, `W_q_gain` is clamped to `[0.1, 1.0]`. This is a multiplicative gain that can only divide the L4→L23E feedforward drive, never amplify it. A sharpening-style mechanism would require, under matched cue, enhancing the pref-of-probe units above their basal drive — i.e., a gain > 1 on those units. The clamp forbids this. Second, the learning rule `ΔW_q_gain[cue, :] = -η · cue_on · r_l23e · sign(matched)` has a pre-factor of `r_l23e` that is largest on units that already respond strongly to the cued orientation; the sign of `Δ` is negative on matched trials. So the learned gain is driven down fastest on exactly the pref-of-cue units, and any unit whose r_l23e is small (nonpref) gets negligible update. Both the parametric clamp and the Hebbian-style update independently force the gain to carve a suppression trough on pref-of-cue, not a peak. The post-training weights confirm this at the limit of the clamp: `0.10` on pref, `0.71` on nonpref, `1.0` everywhere else. The β mechanism as built has no path to produce a gain > 1 on any unit under any learning trajectory, so it has no path to produce a Kok-style sharpening signature in which the pref response to an expected stimulus is enhanced above baseline.

## 6. The subtractive-apical (Option 2) toy test result

Before proposing an Option-2 build that replaces the multiplicative β with a subtractive-apical `r_j = softplus(b_j(probe) − a_j(cue))`, the toy script `scripts/v2/toy_alpha_subtractive.py` asked whether that mechanism can produce both directions on hand-crafted apical patterns. Pattern A — apical carries the pref-pattern `α · exp(−Δθ²/2σ²)` peaked on pref-of-cue — gave a clean dampening signature: at `α ∈ {0.3, 0.5, 1.0, 1.5}` all three triad criteria passed simultaneously (mean_r_match < mean_r_mismatch, pref/nonpref_match < pref/nonpref_mismatch, and Δacc ≤ 0, sliding from 0 at α=0.3 down to −1 at α=1.5). Pattern B — apical carries the inverted nonpref-pattern `α · (1 − exp(−Δθ²/2σ²))` peaked on nonpref-of-cue — produced the right pref/nonpref asymmetry (ratio rises on matched, falls on mismatched, Δacc becomes strongly positive) at every `α > 0` but failed criterion 1 (mean_r_match > mean_r_mismatch at every α). Pattern C — the lead-proposed mixed form `α₀ + α₁ · (1 − exp(−Δθ²/2σ²))` swept over `α₀ ∈ {0.0, 0.1, 0.3, 0.5}` and `α₁ ∈ {0.3, 0.5, 1.0, 1.5}` — gave 16 cells, not one of which satisfied the strict sharpening triad. The uniform α₀ floor lowers every unit's pre-softplus drive by the same constant and so does not change the matched-vs-mismatched ordering — it just lowers both conditions by the same bounded amount.

The structural reason Pattern C fails criterion 1 everywhere is a direct derivation from the symmetry of the nonpref-pattern term. The term `α₁ · (1 − basal(cue, pref_j))` peaks where `basal(cue, pref_j)` is small, i.e., on units whose pref is far from the cue. Under matched (`cue = probe`) those units are nonpref-of-probe and are already near-zero on basal, so subtracting a large apical from them just pushes them deeper into softplus's low-slope region (small population-level effect). Under mismatched (`cue = probe + 90°`) the same term peaks on pref-of-probe units — the ones that ARE firing from basal — and the subtraction collides with the peak of the basal response, producing a big drop. So mismatched total firing is always lower than matched total firing for any ratio-increasing Pattern-C cell. Pattern A has the mirror-image algebra: its pref-pattern apical peaks on pref-of-cue, which is pref-of-probe under matched (big collision) and nonpref-of-probe under mismatched (small effect), so matched < mismatched in total firing. Any linear subtractive apical that raises pref/nonpref ratio on matched necessarily pairs that with matched > mismatched in total firing. The two signatures are welded together in opposite directions by the linear algebra of the subtraction.

## 7. Current status

The Kok sharpening signature is not reachable with the mechanisms we have built (β / W_q_gain multiplicative gate clamped at unity) nor with the most natural biological extension of those mechanisms (subtractive apical with cue-conditional prediction patterns). What we have, end-to-end validated, is a clean dampening signature: on the learned β checkpoint, matched cue suppresses pref-of-probe L2/3E activity 10× and flips the decoder class axis; dampening is reproduced robustly by energy and shape metrics and by frozen-localizer fMRI-style decoding. What we do not have is a path to the Kok sharpening sign through these mechanisms. The remaining candidates are, in increasing architectural weight: (a) extend Pattern A with a cue-specific noise reduction on the basal drive before the softplus, so the shrinkage lowers noise variance on pref-of-probe units more than on nonpref — this would potentially carry a ratio increase without flipping the total-firing sign because the mean is unaffected by variance reduction; (b) combine a subtractive apical with a multiplicative attention boost (`W_attend[cue, :]` with gain clamp > 1) on the same L23E pathway — this requires two independent weight matrices but lets the learning rule choose sign per cell; (c) an architecturally different mechanism, e.g. a layer-6 gating circuit that re-routes cue information through a separate population before subtraction. None of these should be built without a fresh toy test that demonstrates the sharpening criteria are mechanically reachable, in the spirit of the toy test we just ran.

## 8. What is validated end-to-end

The pieces of the V2 stack that are currently green and safe to build on, from bottom to top: the LGN front-end (Gabor even/odd + DoG kernel stack, frozen hashes retained in checkpoint `frozen_sha`) and the Levels 1 through 10 diagnostics that exercise each successive stage on controlled stimuli; the phase-2 predictive training pipeline in `scripts/v2/train_phase2_predictive.py` (30-step forward-only warmup, 50-step soft-reset segments, batch size 4 persistent worlds, Urbanczik–Senn on L4→L23E, Vogels iSTDP on all I→E routes, three-factor Hebbian on the context-memory generic weights, ThresholdHomeostasis with adaptive θ per population under a tanh + deadband guard); the Dale's-law-respecting excitatory/inhibitory split applied at forward time via `_excitatory_eff`/`_inhibitory_eff`; the fast softplus activations on excitatory populations and ReLU threshold on inhibitory populations; the sparse retinotopic top-k orientation-biased feedforward mask (`_build_l4_l23_mask`, Fix K — every L23E unit receives `round(0.12 × n_l4_e) = 15` L4 afferents centred on a target retino cell and biased toward a target pref orientation); the energy penalty (α_rate L1 and β_syn implicit-Euler shrinkage with per-weight `raw_prior` anchors from Fix P); `eval_kok` with the new `--probe-cue-gate` flag and the new `_install_learned_w_q_gain` helper that mirrors `step3_beta_level11`'s loader; the fMRI-style pseudo-voxel decoder with `--cue-mode ∈ {none, expected, unexpected}` and `--protocol frozen-localizer`; the Level 11 expectation-sign gate; the feedback-vs-decoder isolation harness; and the toy_alpha_subtractive mechanism sweep over Patterns A, B, C.

## 9. What is NOT validated / open issues

Nothing on the Richter side is evaluated on this substrate — `scripts/v2/train_phase3_richter_learning.py` exists and is structurally analogous to the Kok trainer but has not been run on the validated phase-2 checkpoint, so `scripts/v2/eval_richter.py` has no beta-equivalent checkpoint to read. The three plastic-manifest tests (`tests/v2/test_layers_load_bearing_phase_split.py::test_phase3_kok_all_frozen[L23E-…]`, `test_plastic_and_frozen_cover_all_weights`, and `tests/v2/test_network_plastic_weight_names.py::test_phase3_kok_only_task_weights`) are failing in the working tree because `W_q_gain` was added to the phase-3 Kok plastic manifest during Task #74 Step-3 but the expected-set literals in those tests were not updated — `git stash` confirms they pass on the committed code, so the failure is pre-existing tech debt from the prior session, not caused by any patch landed in this session. The fine-discrim eval head inside `eval_kok.evaluate_kok` is design-broken at the current operating point: with `n_localizer_orients = 12` the decoder's labels sit on a 15° grid, and any `fine-offset-deg` below 7.5° produces a tolerance narrower than the half-grid so the classifier structurally cannot score any trial correct; this needs a redesign toward either a circular-regression read-out or a denser localizer (36 orients → 5° grid → 2.5° tolerance becomes at least measurable). Finally, and most importantly for this task line: no sharpening mechanism candidate we have tried survives bottom-up verification. The mechanism search should not treat the β dampening result as either "the right answer" or "a failure to fix"; the right framing is that the subtractive/multiplicative one-pathway mechanism class is structurally incapable of producing both signs of the expectation effect, and a qualitatively new mechanism (noise-based denoising, two-pathway attention, or a separate layer-6 gate) is needed before phase-3 training can be repeated with a sharpening-capable architecture.
